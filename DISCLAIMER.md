# Disclaimer

**READ CAREFULLY BEFORE USE.**

This tool is an **educational re-implementation** of the UK RCLEA (Radioactively Contaminated Land Exposure Assessment) methodology. It is intended for:

- Self-study and learning about radiological risk assessment;
- Training courses in environmental health, contaminated-land, and radiological protection;
- Demonstrating the structure of the Part 2A statutory framework;
- Exploring the quantitative behaviour of the RCLEA model (sensitivity, extensibility).

## What this tool is NOT

- **Not a regulatory tool.** It must not be used as the sole basis for decisions under Part 2A of the Environmental Protection Act 1990, the Radioactive Contaminated Land (Enabling Powers) (England) Regulations 2005, or any equivalent regime in Scotland, Wales, Northern Ireland, or any other jurisdiction.
- **Not endorsed** by the UK Environment Agency, UKHSA, DEFRA, or any other authority.
- **Not a substitute for qualified professional assessment.** Radiological risk assessment of contaminated land requires a Radiation Protection Adviser, an experienced radiological consultant, or the relevant statutory body.
- **Not exhaustive.** The model covers a fixed set of 47 radionuclides, 4 land-use scenarios, and 6 exposure pathways. Real sites may have contaminants, pathways, or receptors outside this scope.
- **Not guaranteed accurate.** Conservative default assumptions may over- or under-estimate real risk. Dose coefficients evolve with ICRP recommendations.

## Using outputs responsibly

When using this tool in an educational setting:

1. Treat every output as illustrative, not definitive.
2. Always cross-reference with the published methodology (CRCE-RAD-003-2020, CLR-13 "Using RCLEA", and the Part 2A Statutory Guidance).
3. Never present output to a regulator, client, or the public as a formal assessment.
4. When in doubt, consult a qualified radiological practitioner.
